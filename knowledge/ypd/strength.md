## SPEED DEVELOPMENT

**目標（Objectives）**
- Improve linear sprinting speed

- Develop appropriate running technique for both acceleration and maximal velocity through a range of technical drills and sprint based activities.

- Provide regular exposure to linear speed to maintain speed qualities throughout the year & prevent injury

**成果（Outcomes）**
Athletes should all be able to:

- Demonstrate good technique in  line with the technical model for acceleration*

- Demonstrate good technique in  line with the technical model for maximal velocity*

- Perform basic technical running  drills with a high level of competency

- Describe the key technical features of acceleration and maximal velocity running technique using the PAL paradigm.

*At this stage it is not realistic to expect excellent technique as the underpinning physical qualities (e.g. con/ecc strength, power, reactive strength) are not developed sufficiently to support optimal mechanics.

INTRODUCTION

Speed is KING.

Speed WINS.

These are common phrases you will hear around performance sport.

The athletes at the top of the food chain are fast, and the good news is that despite truly elite speed capabilities being a genetic gift, all athletes can get faster (relatively) with a well-designed training plan that addresses the key components.

Young athletes will naturally get faster through the process of maturation due to favorable changes in factors including muscle and tendon physiology and changes in limb length allowing them to generate more torque.

Mass:

The athlete needs an appropriate (not excessive) level of muscle for their sport/position, and a low level of body fat mass*.

In elite sprinting, the athletes need to be like a formula one car incredibly powerful engines, but a relatively lightweight body to maximize acceleration and top end speed.

In sports like Rugby Union or Rugby League it is advantageous to carry more mass because of the collision component of the games. So it is necessary, to aim to increase body mass to some extent, but if we take hypertrophy too far this can negatively affect speed performance.

## PHASE 1 - STRENGTH DEVELOPMENT

**目標（Objectives）**
- Develop technique through full ranges in basic strength exercises vs. bodyweight

- Optimise muscular balance through well rounded strength programming

- Develop solid base of general strength and stability

- Establish a sound technique in general strength exercises vs. low loads ready for next phase

- Develop knowledge of how to train safely & effectively in a gym environment

**成果（Outcomes）**
- ALL ATHLETES SHOULD BE ABLE TO:

- Describe the benefits of strength training to health & performance

- Describe the components of a full strength session: warm up, jumps, resistance training, cool down

- Demonstrate the ability to listen to and follow the coach/teacher’s instructions

- Use the gym and it’s equipment safely and effectively including adjusting equipment and spotting correctly

- Perform Squat, Lunge, Push, Pull, Hinge, Brace & Rotate movements with excellent technique, identifying the key teaching points for each.

- Demonstrate a high level of trunk strength and stability

- Work through a programme in a training pair, providing appropriate support & encouragement

- Show a high level of gym etiquette including tidying up equipment for the next session

## PHASE 3: LINEAR & LATERAL CHANGE OF DIRECTION / AGILITY

**目標（Objectives）**
- Develop a broad range of sports generic movement skills relating to changing direction/agility

- Develop an understanding of different target functions of the movements – i.e. when and where they may be utilized in different sports

- Aim to master the correct technique under simple, closed conditions that can be built upon in the subsequent phases

- Engage the athletes through games that will also provide an opportunity to contextualise the movement skills in the athletes’ sport(s)

**成果（Outcomes）**
All athletes should be able to:

- Perform all of the sports generic movement skills with good technique under closed conditions

- Describe the key technical points for the movements that may include simple external coaching cues

- Identify situations in their sports where these movements occur and what the movement helps them to achieve (e.g. The ready position allows them to move quickly in any direction)

INTRODUCTION

The above flow diagram & session plan outline the way that we have integrated the sports generic movement games in with the closed & open drills. We set the context and engage the athletes with the first game situation at the end of a RAMP warm up that incorporates bodyweight dynamic flexibility and strength movements and some multi-planar jumps.

During the game as the coach/teacher you need to pay close attention to the target movements for the session (in the examples below linear acceleration and lateral movements) and look for what the athletes are doing well and what needs improving in the technique. Then break out into the simplified drill scenarios to improve the technical execution of the target movements. Based on what you see you may need to spend more or less time on certain drills depending on the needs of your athletes.

By following the progressions through these sessions below the athletes will be exposed to a range of sports generic movements under varying conditions, that we can continue to build upon with exposure during warm ups in subsequent training phases in the current year and in the phases beyond. We are not expecting perfect movement at the Foundation stage but they should understand the basic technical requirements of each movement and display a good level of competence when executing them.

It is really important to challenge the athletes to think about where these movements occur in their sports, what their function is and when they may be most appropriately applied. Rest periods between the short, high intensity efforts within the session as you transition between games & drills, or between drills is an ideal time for this through a simple Q&A scenario.

## PHASE 4: STRENGTH & POWER DEVELOPMENT II

**目標（Objectives）**
- Engage the athletes – continue to make links between movements in the gym and movements in their sport

- Progress complexity and volume of strength exercises inline with strength training matrix A & B

- Introduce multi-planar jumps to learn to produce force and control landings in different planes of movement

- Introduce low amplitude fast SSC plyometrics to build elastic qualities in the lower body

- Establish routines in the gym environment – setting up equipment, partners supporting, peer coaching key technical points & putting equipment away

**成果（Outcomes）**
- Demonstrate technical competence with more advanced levels in the movement matrix (look for individual progression up the levels).

- Execute the full range of multi-planar jumps and be able to stick the landings (hold 2-3 secs).

- Perform low amplitude plyometrics, demonstrating fast contact times with minimal angular displacement at the ankle, knee and hip on contact and dorsi-flexed ankle position to ‘reload’ in flight.

- Demonstrate developing competence in the simple speed skills inline with the technical models for each.

- Work in the S&C gym environment with a training partner and demonstrate good levels of gym etiquette (e.g. tidying the gym, putting equipment away when finished)

## INTEGRATED RAMP WARM UPS

**內容摘要**
INTEGRATED RAMP WARM UPS
(AN INTEGRATED APPROACH TO YOUTH PHYSICAL DEVELOPMENT FOR PHYSICAL EDUCATION & YOUTH SPORTS COACHES)

When contact time is limited within a teaching or coaching environment, short but frequent exposures to different physical training stimuli at the start of a lesson / coaching session is an effective approach for young students/athletes to diversify their development beyond the technical & tactical domain.

Whilst short when viewed in isolation (10-15 mins) the sum of that across the course of a year is huge.

Based on 2 x PE/Sports sessions per week, for 39 weeks of the year (UK School Year) this amounts to a total of 780 minutes or 13 hours of physical development, that will complement and enhance your technical and tactical development.

RAMP WARMS UPS

The RAMP warm up system consists of 4 phases:

Raise body temperature & heart rate
Activate key muscle groups
Mobilise joints
Potentiate (power up) with high intensity, explosive movements
